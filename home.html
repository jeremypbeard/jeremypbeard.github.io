About me

I am a fifth year Mathematics PhD candidate at Carnegie Mellon University, advised by Rami Grossberg. My research is in logic, specifically model theory, with a focus on abstract elementary classes.

Recent news

  • I have an recently published paper with Marcos Mazari-Armida, On the spectrum of limit models (Annals of Pure and Applied Logic, Volume 176, Issue 10, 2025). We prove that in a nice enough AEC with a well behaved independence relation, long limit models are isomorphic, and short limit models are distinct:

    Published version PDF arXiv.

  • I gave a talk about this paper at the 2025 Logic Colloquium in Vienna:

    Slides.

  • I have an upcoming paper, The spectrum of limit models in a first order setting, which greatly simplifies the proof of the main result of On the spectrum of limit models when we restrict to first order model theory:

    PDF arXiv.

  • And another upcoming paper, Long limit models are isomorphic assuming a splitting-like relation, which generalises (to my knowledge) all positive isomorphism results of limit models in AECs with amalgamation, including the main result of Chapter 3 of On the spectrum of limit models:

    PDF, arXiv.
