Published work

  • On the spectrum of limit models, joint work with Marcos Mazari-Armida

    Annals of Pure and Applied Logic, Volume 176, Issue 10, 2025.

    We prove that in a nice enough AEC with a well behaved independence relation, long limit models are isomorphic, and short limit models are distinct. Combining these we get easy-to-use 'black box' global results that characterise all spectra limit models in all large enough stable cardinals, and provide concrete examples of AECs our results apply to. Some proofs removed from the published version are present in Version 1.

    Published version, PDF, arXiv.

Preprints

  • The spectrum of limit models in a first order setting

    We give a much simpler proof of the main result of On the spectrum of limit models in the context of first order model theory. All definitions and results are presented in the language of first order model theory, which we hope makes the text more accessible to readers with little to no knowledge of AECs and limit models.

    PDF, arXiv.

  • Long limit models are isomorphic assuming a splitting-like relation

    We prove that in an AEC with some amalgamation and a fairly weak independence relation, limit models of cofinality higher than the local character cardinal are isomorphic. To my knowledge, this generalises all positive isomorphism results of limit models in AECs with amalgamation, including the main result in Chapter 3 of On the spectrum of limit models.

    PDF, arXiv.

  • Disjoint non-forking amalgamation in strictly stable AECs

    We prove that in a nice AEC with a somewhat well behaved independence relation (assuming only weak forms of uniqueness, existence, and non-forking amalgamation), all high cofinality limit models are disjoint amalgamation bases. We even show that, if the 'weak' properties are strengthened, we can also guarantee independence of given singletons over opposite models in the disjoint amalgamation..

    PDF, arXiv.
